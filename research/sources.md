## 1. Kyle Poyar — Growth Unhinged

- Focus: B2B SaaS growth, PLG, newsletter-as-growth
- LinkedIn: [link]
- YouTube / podcast: [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RX68Jm556Ao](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RX68Jm556Ao)]
- Collected: LinkedIn posts [ ] | Transcripts [x]
- Publish date: 2025
- MY TAKE: The welcome email stood out to me because it’s the point where subscribers are most interested in learning more. It’s the best opportunity to introduce what the newsletter is about, set expectations, and guide them on what to do next. I also liked the suggestion to ask readers to reply or move the email to their Primary inbox. I hadn’t thought about how those simple actions also improve email deliverability. For B2B SaaS, I can see this working well as a GoHighLevel welcome sequence where contacts are tagged based on whether they reply, making it easier to personalize future follow-ups.

## 2. Matt McGarry — Newsletter Operator

- Focus: Newsletter growth & monetization (acquisition mechanics)
- LinkedIn: [link]
- YouTube / podcast: [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aAmm5Bm_odI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aAmm5Bm_odI), [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZllqhESam1k](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZllqhESam1k)]
- Language: English
- Collected: LinkedIn posts [ ] | Transcripts [x]
- Publish date: 2025
- MY TAKE: One thing I liked about McGarry’s 1K Challenge is that while it’s intentionally manual, a lot of the process can still be systemized. Creating a 30-day content calendar, scheduling weekly teaser posts, and setting up follow-up reminders can all be automated. I would keep the LinkedIn outreach manual though since automated DMs violate LinkedIn’s terms. The biggest takeaway for me was his point that open and click rates can be misleading because of bots. Instead of focusing on vanity metrics, I’d build a pipeline that tracks where qualified leads and revenue are actually coming from. That gives a much clearer picture of what’s working and what should be scaled.
- MY TAKE: This ties in with my other takeaway from McGarry’s challenge. I liked how he ranked more than 30 acquisition channels based on conversions and revenue instead of just subscriber growth. That’s exactly how I would build it by tagging every subscriber based on where they came from, then tracking which sources actually generate buyers and revenue. Another good point was regularly cleaning the email list by removing subscribers who stay inactive after several emails. That improves deliverability and reinforces the idea that engagement signals matter. I also found his list of the worst-performing channels useful because it helps identify what isn’t worth spending time building in the first place.

## 4. Val Geisler — lifecycle / retention email

- Focus: Lifecycle email, engagement-first segmentation (ex-Klaviyo, Digioh)
- LinkedIn: [link]
- YouTube / podcast: [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C4DKWaoGENw](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C4DKWaoGENw)]
- Collected: LinkedIn posts [ ] | Transcripts [x]
- Publish date: 2020
- MY TAKE: The biggest takeaway for me was Geisler’s point that lifecycle emails should be behavior-based instead of time-based. Customers should receive emails based on what they actually do inside the product, not because it’s Day 3 or Day 7. That’s exactly how I would build it in GoHighLevel or n8n using event-driven workflows. I also liked her idea of tracking customers who reply to onboarding emails. I would take it a step further by using that as an engagement signal, tagging those contacts in the CRM, and incorporating it into a customer health score alongside LTV and churn risk. Even though this is an older podcast, the idea of behavior-based automation is still one of the best practices for building lifecycle workflows.

## 5. Jay Schwedelson — Do This, Not That

- Focus: Email tactics — subject lines, deliverability, send behavior
- LinkedIn: [link]
- YouTube / podcast: [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zKrjlaSiIDg](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zKrjlaSiIDg)]
- Collected: LinkedIn posts [ ] | Transcripts [x]
- Publish date: 2025 
- MY TAKE: Schwedelson’s most valuable insight for me was the shift in Apple Mail. Confirmation-style subject lines can now increase the chances of landing in the Priority inbox, making it a practical deliverability test rather than just another copywriting experiment. I also liked his perspective on AI: use it as a research assistant, not a copywriter. That’s exactly how I’d build it into a workflow, letting AI handle competitor analysis, Reddit research, and newsletter discovery while keeping strategy, judgment, and final messaging human.

## 6. Katelyn Bourgoin — Why We Buy

- Focus: Buyer psychology applied to email copy & subject lines
- LinkedIn: [link]
- YouTube / podcast: [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V4SHt99AXqM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V4SHt99AXqM)]
- Collected: LinkedIn posts [ ] | Transcripts [x]
- Publish date: 2022
- MY TAKE: Bourgoin’s core message was to understand *why* a cognitive bias works, not just that it boosts conversions. For me, that’s where it connects to automation. Biases like scarcity, reciprocity, and social proof become behavior-based triggers that are applied based on where someone is in the customer journey—not tactics sent to everyone. That’s how I think about building smarter lifecycle campaigns.
