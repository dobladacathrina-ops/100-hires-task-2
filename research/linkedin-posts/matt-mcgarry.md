## Post 1

- **URL:** [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/matt-mcgarry_hot-take-linkedin-newsletters-are-dead-share-7372393276814966784-9CUf](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/matt-mcgarry_hot-take-linkedin-newsletters-are-dead-share-7372393276814966784-9CUf/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- **Posted:** September 2025

**MY TAKE:** McGarry argues that LinkedIn newsletters aren’t the best way to grow an audience. Instead, he recommends using lead magnets, comment-to-get campaigns, and clear CTAs to move people from LinkedIn to an owned email list. I agree with the overall strategy because the goal is to build an audience you own, not one that’s tied to a platform. The comment-to-get approach also becomes much more valuable when it’s connected to an automated workflow. A comment can trigger a DM with the lead magnet, tag the new contact, and enroll them in a welcome sequence. That creates a smooth path from social engagement to email without adding manual work. The only point I’d be cautious about is the claim that LinkedIn newsletters are “dead.” His experience gives that argument credibility, but it’s still based on observation rather than broad evidence. I’d frame it as a strategy that hasn’t consistently delivered strong results rather than a universal rule.
