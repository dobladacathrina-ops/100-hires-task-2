**Note:** This post is lifecycle-adjacent. It starts with email and lifecycle marketing, then shifts to the gap between the inbox and the onsite experience. I’m including it because the first half is directly relevant to lifecycle email, and my take focuses on that email-to-website handoff.

**My take:** Geisler’s point is that personalization shouldn’t end when someone clicks an email. The email knows who the customer is, but the website often forgets and treats everyone the same. I think the solution is carrying that context through the click. Someone who already joined your list shouldn’t see another email signup popup, and a repeat customer shouldn’t be shown a first-time buyer offer. That’s where automation ties everything together. The email click should pass the customer’s context so the website can continue the conversation instead of starting over.
